Who we are

We are Business Development Services Provider, offering a range of business support options and solutions, tailored to your needs, at any stage of your business growth. We are registered and licensed to operate in Tanzania. Our desire to help you achieve business growth and sustainable performance, together with the gaps we identified is the reason for our existence.

Our Strategic Pillars

In executing its vision and purpose, ADC has developed its 3-year Strategic Plan which is defined by three (3) key strategic pillars. These are

Key Player in Financial Sector transformation by providing solutions to banks and financial institutions

Key player in SME Business Growth by providing solutions to SMEs particularly women and youth

Drive digitalization of Businesses for efficiency through providing digital solutions to various businesses

What We Offer

Capacity Building

ADC Tanzania Limited is comprised of seasoned bankers with experience in both local and international banking industry, including roles in the central bank (Bank of Tanzania), commercial banks, development financial institutions, and microfinance institutions. ADC's training and development approach includes both classroom and virtual formats, enriched by case studies from local and foreign businesses.

Project Partners in Action

ADC Tanzania has been collaborating with like-minded development partners such as the government, USADF, ACELI AFRICA, AGF and private sector to support and implement various initiatives and programs targeting MSMEs and small holder farmers in agribusiness value chains and other economic sectors.

United States African Development Foundation (USADF)

Collaborating with ADC to implement programs that promote the development of smallholder agricultural producer groups.

ACELI Africa

Collaborating with ADC to deliver business development services (BDS) to SME’s engaged in agricultural value chains.

African Guarantee Fund (AGF)

ADC has partnered with AGF under the AFAWA program to support women-led SMEs by providing capacity-building services and access to finance.

Aceli Africa

As partners, ADC plays a crucial role in facilitating consultations and offering business development services aimed at enhancing the services offered by Aceli Africa to SMEs. This effort contributes to the advancement of several Sustainable Development Goals, including the promotion of livelihoods for farmers and workers, fostering climate-smart and resilient agriculture, promoting gender inclusion, and ensuring food security and nutrition in Africa.

  • Conducting Fully funded diagnostic to determine core business needs, Based on diagnostic, the service provider and SME agree on a scope of work and budget for a 3–6-month engagement
  • Business contributes 20-30% to the budget and Aceli contributes maximum of US $15,000 to the budget

  • Annual revenue of US $400,000 to US $5,000,000
  • 15+ full-time employees
  • Been in operation for at least 3 years
  • Primary motivation: sustain growth/ attract investment
